# Transparency - List of Manipulations and Bias

## Manipulation techniques and psychological effects:

1. **Door in the Face**: Make a big request that will be rejected, then make a smaller request that is more likely to be accepted.
2. **Hoovering**: Emotional manipulation to win someone back, e.g., through guilt or false promises.
3. **Zeigarnik effect**: Incomplete tasks remain in the memory, increasing motivation to complete them.
4. **Sense of scarcity**: Suggesting scarcity to increase urgency and demand.
5. **Placebo**: Belief in the effect (e.g., of a pill) leads to an actual effect, even though no substance is working.
6. **Framing Effect**: The presentation of information influences decisions, e.g., positive vs. negative wording.
7. **Emotional Contagious Exploitation**: The emotions of others are exploited to manipulate mood.
8. **Mirror Neurons Exploit**: Imitation of behavior/feelings to generate trust or sympathy.
9. **Divide and Conquer**: Dividing a group to maintain control or exploit weaknesses.
10. **Loss Aversion**: Fear of loss is perceived more strongly than joy at gain, influencing decisions.
11. **Priming Effect**: Previous stimuli unconsciously influence later perceptions/decisions.
12. **Power of Touch**: Touch creates trust or subtly influences decisions.
13. **Halo Effect**: Positive characteristics (e.g., appearance) color the overall impression.
14. **Low Balling**: An enticing offer that is later worsened, but acceptance remains due to commitment.
15. **Cognitive Dissonance**: Discomfort caused by contradictory thoughts/actions leads to justification.
16. **Groupthink Induction**: A group is pressured into uniform thinking, often through pressure or isolation.
17. **Confirmation Bias Reinforcement**: The tendency to seek only information that confirms existing beliefs.
18. **Illusion of Choice**: A false choice in which options are designed to achieve the desired result.
19. **Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP)**: Language and behavior patterns used to influence people subconsciously.
20. **Sense of Urgency**: Creating time pressure to encourage quick, ill-considered decisions.
21. **Baader-Meinhof phenomenon**: After first encountering something, it suddenly seems to appear everywhere.
22. **Subliminal messaging**: Subconscious messages through barely perceptible stimuli.
23. **Foot in the mouth**: Using embarrassing statements or behavior to gain control.
24. **Playing dumb**: Pretending to be stupid to elicit information or avoid responsibility.
25. **Love bombing**: Excessive affection/approval to create trust or dependence.
26. **Bandwagon effect manipulation**: Exploiting the tendency to go along with the majority.
27. **Reframing Failure**: Reinterpreting failure as an opportunity or learning process to promote acceptance.
28. **Gaslighting**: Making victims doubt their own perceptions to gain control.
29. **Guilt Tripping**: Inducing feelings of guilt to control behavior.
30. **Contrast Principle**: Presenting options in such a way that one appears better in comparison.
31. **Emotional Blackmail**: Emotional blackmail through threats or guilt.
32. **Brainwashing**: Long-term manipulation to change beliefs/values.
33. **Insecurity Exploitation**: Exploiting insecurities to gain control or influence.
34. **Cultivating Dependence**: Encouraging dependence to gain power over someone.
35. **Triangulation**: Involving a third party to create jealousy, competition, or control.
36. **Authority Bias**: Exploiting trust in authority figures to encourage obedience.
37. **Mind Control**: Extreme manipulation to completely control thoughts/behavior.
38. **Cultivating False Identity**: Promoting a false identity or role to maintain control.
39. **Social Proof Exploit**: Exploiting the urge to follow the behavior of others.
40. **Sense of Obligation**: Creating a sense of duty to force actions.
41. **Stockholm Syndrome**: Victims develop sympathy for perpetrators through dependence or trauma.
42. **Learned Helplessness**: Belief that one has no control leads to passivity.
43. **Fear Mongering**: Stirring up fear to force control or obedience.
44. **Memory Manipulation**: Distorting memories or implanting false memories.
45. **Public Shaming**: Public humiliation to control behavior.
46. **Scapegoating**: Blaming a person/group to exonerate others.
47. **Seed of Self-Doubt**: Sowing doubt to undermine self-confidence.
48. **Exploiting Empathy**: Exploiting compassion to gain advantages.
49. **Micro Manipulation**: Subtle, small manipulations that add up.
50. **Exploiting Need for Closure**: Exploiting the urge for closure to force quick decisions.
51. **Foot in the Door**: Make a small request in order to gain greater acceptance later on.
52. **Suggestive Language and Hypnosis**: Language or techniques used to influence people subconsciously.
53. **Pacing and Leading**: Build trust (pacing), then guide (leading).
54. **Anchoring Bias Exploitation**: Use initial information as a reference to influence judgments.
55. **Pygmalion Effect**: Expectations of a person influence their performance.
56. **Time Constraints**: Use time pressure to prevent rational decisions.
57. **Decoy Effect**: Introduce an unattractive option to make others seem more attractive.
58. **IKEA Effect**: Things you have worked for yourself are valued more highly.
59. **Endowment Effect**: Possessions are overvalued simply because you have them.
60. **Reciprocity Principle**: Do someone a favor or give them a gift to create an obligation to reciprocate.
61. **Commitment and Consistency**: People strive to act consistently with previous decisions, which is exploited by previous commitments.
62. **Bait and Switch**: A bait offer that is replaced by a less attractive one after interest has been aroused.
63. **Sunk Cost Fallacy**: Continuing to invest in something (time, money) because a lot has already been invested, even if it is unreasonable.
64. **Barnum Effect**: Perceiving general statements as personally applicable, often used in horoscopes or personality tests.
65. **Overton Window**: Shifting the frame of acceptable opinions to make extreme views seem normal.
66. **FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)**: Fear of missing out is exploited to encourage quick decisions.
67. **Salience Bias**: Focusing on salient or emotionally charged information that overshadows other factors.
68. **Availability Heuristic**: Overestimating the probability of events based on readily available memories.
69. **Mere Exposure Effect**: Repeated exposure leads to greater sympathy for a person, idea, or thing.
70. **Chameleon Effect**: Unconsciously imitating the behavior of others to gain sympathy or trust.
71. **False Consensus Effect**: Assuming that one's own beliefs/behaviors are shared by the majority.
72. **Self-Fulfilling Prophecy**: Expectations lead to behaviors that confirm those expectations.
73. **Projection**: Transferring one's own unwanted feelings or characteristics onto others in order to manipulate them.
74. **Deindividuation**: In groups, people lose their individuality, which can lead to manipulable behavior.
75. **Reverse Psychology**: Getting someone to do the opposite of what is suggested by forbidding it.
76. **Distraction Technique**: Using distraction to circumvent critical thinking or influence actions.
78. **False Dichotomy**: Presenting only two options to steer a decision in a certain direction.
79. **Herd Mentality**: Similar to the bandwagon effect, but with a stronger focus on unconsciously following the crowd.
80. **Primacy/Recency Effect**: First (primacy) or last (recency) information has a greater influence on perception.
